A-Level Mathematics Worksheet: Reverse Chain Rule

Diagrams, Graphs and Images

The diagram shows a circle of radius \( r = 4 \) cm inscribed in a square. Find the exact area of the region inside the square but outside the circle, and evaluate it to 1 decimal place. 3 marks

The graph shows the curve \( y=(x-2)(x+3) \) and the line \( y=3-x \). Find the exact coordinates of their points of intersection. 4 marks

The logo shown is printed 6 cm wide. Its width-to-height ratio is 4 : 3. Find the printed height and the printed area. 2 marks

Core Practice

Use the reverse chain rule. Include the constant of integration where appropriate.

Find \( \displaystyle \int (x+2)(x^2+4x+9)^5 \, dx \). 3 marks

Find \( \displaystyle \int \cos x \sqrt{4+\sin x} \, dx \). 3 marks

Find \( \displaystyle \int \frac{\sin x}{2+\cos x} \, dx \). 3 marks

Definite Integrals

Give exact answers, simplifying logarithms where possible.

Calculate \( \displaystyle \int_0^2 4x(x^2+1)^3 \, dx \). 4 marks

Calculate \( \displaystyle \int_0^{\pi/4} 6\sec^2 x\, e^{3\tan x} \, dx \). 4 marks

Show that \( \displaystyle \int_{\pi/6}^{\pi/3} \frac{2\sin x}{1+\cos x}\,dx = 2\ln(1+\sqrt{3}/2)-2\ln(3/2) \). 5 marks

Worked Answers

These solutions are included after the questions and can also receive page breaks.

The square has side \(2r = 8\) cm, so its area is \(64\) cm\(^2\); the circle has area \(\pi r^2 = 16\pi\) cm\(^2\).

Shaded area \(= 64 - 16\pi \approx 13.7\) cm\(^2\) (1 d.p.).

At an intersection \((x-2)(x+3) = 3-x\), so \(x^2+x-6 = 3-x\), giving \(x^2+2x-9 = 0\) and \(x = -1 \pm \sqrt{10}\).

Using \(y = 3-x\), the points are \((-1+\sqrt{10},\, 4-\sqrt{10}) \approx (2.16,\, 0.84)\) and \((-1-\sqrt{10},\, 4+\sqrt{10}) \approx (-4.16,\, 7.16)\).

Height \(= 6 \times \tfrac{3}{4} = 4.5\) cm, so the printed area is \(6 \times 4.5 = 27\) cm\(^2\).

Let \(u=x^2+5\), so \(du=2x\,dx\).

\(\displaystyle \int 2x(x^2+5)^4\,dx=\int u^4\,du=\frac{1}{5}(x^2+5)^5+c\).

Let \(u=x^3-1\), so \(du=3x^2\,dx\).

\(\displaystyle \int 3x^2e^{x^3-1}\,dx=e^{x^3-1}+c\).

Let \(u=3x^2+7\), so \(du=6x\,dx\).

\(\displaystyle \int \frac{6x}{3x^2+7}\,dx=\ln|3x^2+7|+c\).

Let \(u=x^2+4x+9\), so \(du=2(x+2)\,dx\).

\(\displaystyle \int (x+2)(x^2+4x+9)^5\,dx=\frac{1}{2}\int u^5\,du=\frac{1}{12}(x^2+4x+9)^6+c\).

Let \(u=4+\sin x\), so \(du=\cos x\,dx\).

\(\displaystyle \int \cos x\sqrt{4+\sin x}\,dx=\int u^{1/2}\,du=\frac{2}{3}(4+\sin x)^{3/2}+c\).

Let \(u=2+\cos x\), so \(du=-\sin x\,dx\).

\(\displaystyle \int \frac{\sin x}{2+\cos x}\,dx=-\ln|2+\cos x|+c\).

Let \(u=x^2+1\), so \(du=2x\,dx\). The factor \(4x\,dx\) is \(2du\).

\(\displaystyle \int_0^2 4x(x^2+1)^3\,dx=2\int_1^5 u^3\,du=\frac{1}{2}(5^4-1^4)=312\).

Let \(u=3\tan x\), so \(du=3\sec^2x\,dx\). The factor \(6\sec^2x\,dx\) is \(2du\).

\(\displaystyle \int_0^{\pi/4}6\sec^2x e^{3\tan x}\,dx=2\int_0^3 e^u\,du=2(e^3-1)\).

Let \(u=1+\cos x\), so \(du=-\sin x\,dx\).

\(\displaystyle \int \frac{2\sin x}{1+\cos x}\,dx=-2\ln|1+\cos x|+c\).

Substitute \(x=\pi/3\) and \(x=\pi/6\) to obtain \( -2\ln(3/2)+2\ln(1+\sqrt{3}/2) \), which is the required result.
